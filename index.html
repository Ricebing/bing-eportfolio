Reflections

Reflection 1: Sustainability and Viable Systems

While running a 500M-parameter language model on my personal computer for research, I unintentionally caused a noticeable increase in household electricity usage. This experience revealed the often-invisible environmental and financial costs of AI systems, even at small scales.

This incident challenged my assumption that sustainability concerns only apply to large-scale systems. I learned that responsible engineering requires awareness of energy consumption, transparency, and consideration of broader impacts. Going forward, I aim to integrate sustainability thinking into both research and development decisions.

Reflection 2: Professional Practice in Intercultural Contexts

As an international student in Australia, I observed that many technically capable peers struggled to access IT internships due to language, cultural expectations, and local experience requirements.

This reflection helped me recognise that professional practice extends beyond technical competence. Communication norms, cultural awareness, and adaptability are critical. I now approach my professional development with greater emphasis on intercultural communication and contextual understanding.
